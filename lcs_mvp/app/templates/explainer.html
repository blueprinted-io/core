What is this system

A plain-language explainer for contributors, reviewers, and administrators.

AI is strongest on established patterns and weakest at novelty. Newly released software, fresh regulatory changes, and emerging ways of working are exactly where hallucination risk is highest, and they are also the highest-priority learning areas for most organisations.

Blueprinted introduces a human-in-the-loop layer between source documentation and AI-generated learning outputs. Human experts structure, validate, and govern the critical knowledge before it is used. The result is faster content production with controlled accuracy, explicit oversight, and full auditability, so organisations in high-risk or regulated environments can use AI output with confidence and traceability.

How it works

Four stages from raw documentation to governed, auditable content.

1

Ingest source documentation

PDF documents and web pages are processed by AI to extract candidate Tasks and Primers. The import pipeline classifies sections, runs extraction, and surfaces candidates for human review. Ingestion produces candidates only; Workflows are never generated automatically.

2

Review and govern

Contributors review AI-extracted candidates, edit them, and submit them for review. Reviewers confirm or return records through the review queue. Each record version is immutable once confirmed.

3

Compose into Workflows

Authors compose confirmed Tasks into Workflows and attach relevant Primers. A Workflow expresses an expert's judgement about how Tasks should be sequenced for a specific operational context.

4

Deliver governed outputs

Confirmed records drive content output, assessments, and assurance evidence. Every output is grounded in approved, versioned content rather than raw LLM generation.

Core record types

Four first-class record types, all following the same governed lifecycle.

Task

The smallest unit of work that changes the world in one observable way.

  • Outcome: the verifiable result when the task is complete.
  • Facts: background knowledge the operator needs before starting.
  • Concepts: the mental model explaining why the procedure works.
  • Steps: the ordered procedure, each with a completion criterion.
  • Dependencies: what must already be true for the task to be possible.

Primer

A standalone conceptual document: the analytical knowledge that makes practitioners competent, not just procedurally capable.

  • Explains the why and how, not the what to do.
  • Covers trade-offs, failure modes, and conditions under which to choose each approach.
  • Optionally includes level variants (100-400) for different depths: Awareness through Mastery.
  • Can be attached to Workflows to provide conceptual grounding alongside procedural steps.

Workflow

An ordered sequence of Tasks that achieves one operational objective.

  • Contains Tasks, not low-level steps. The Workflow expresses sequencing and context.
  • Can reference Primers to surface conceptual knowledge alongside the procedural steps.
  • Always human-composed; never generated automatically during ingestion.

Step

One unit of procedure inside a Task.

  • Text (required): what you are doing.
  • Actions (optional): tool-specific commands or UI paths for execution.
  • Completion (required): how you verify the step succeeded.

Who uses which area

Access is role-based. Most users need only one or two roles.

Viewer

Read-only access to Workflows and confirmed content. No editing or submission.

Contributor

Runs ingestion, creates and edits Tasks, Primers, and Workflows, and submits records for review.

Reviewer

Works the review queue: confirms or returns submitted records with feedback.

Content publisher

Generates and exports content output and delivery artefacts from confirmed records.

Assessment author

Builds assessments linked to governed records. Assessments inherit the provenance of the records they reference.

Audit

Visibility into assurance evidence and the full audit log. Read-only, no operational access.

Admin

Full access including all contributor and reviewer capabilities, plus break-glass operations, user management, domain configuration, and system settings.
